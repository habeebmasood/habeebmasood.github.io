Displacing a 4-Year Partner

CHALLENGE

A leading construction and infrastructure enterprise in Asia had been working with a global technology solutions provider for four years. Their offices were in the same building. The relationship was warm, the inertia was real, and the switching cost was high. There was no obvious reason for them to look elsewhere.

APPROACH

Rather than compete on features or price, I focused entirely on understanding the problems they had stopped talking about. The ones their existing vendor had normalised over four years. I stayed transparent about what we could not do, and demonstrated clearly how we would solve what actually mattered. Throughout the evaluation, I made sure they felt supported, not sold to.

OUTCOME

They switched. A $300K contract, replacing a partner of four years, built on demonstrated value and trust rather than a better brochure.

Closing During COVID

CHALLENGE

A major enterprise in the tyre industry needed a financial and tax platform. Their decision-makers were largely unreachable. They were likely to continue with their existing vendor. COVID had made procurement cycles difficult to navigate across the board, and there was little reason for them to move.

APPROACH

I stayed close to the contacts I had, patiently and genuinely. When it became clear they also lacked a reliable system integration partner, I found them one. It was not my responsibility. I did it anyway, because it was the right thing to do. That single act shifted the dynamic entirely. The people I had built trust with became internal champions who fast-tracked approvals at the height of the pandemic.

OUTCOME

$50K deal closed within the quarter. At renewal, the contract expanded to include sister entities. Trust compounds.

Book
The Psychology of Money
Morgan Housel

Book
The Almanack of Naval Ravikant
Eric Jorgensen
Naval thinks clearly and communicates crisply. The famous Twitter thread first caught my attention. The book confirmed that brevity and depth are not opposites.
Book
Meditations
Marcus Aurelius
A Roman emperor's private notes, never meant to be published. Two thousand years later, they still hold. Currently re-reading.
Book
The Midnight Library
Matt Haig
One of the best reads of the last five years. A story about regret, choice, and the lives we do not live.
Book
Our Trees Still Grow in Dehra
Ruskin Bond
The bard of the Himalayas. Bond's prose is quiet and precise. He offers the hills to you as if they belong to both of you.
Book
The Forty Rules of Love
Elif Shafak
The great Rumi, Shams of Tabrizi, and how some of the greatest poetry known to mankind came into being.
Book
Delhi by Heart
Raza Rumi
A diplomat's take on one of the world's most chaotic and enigmatic cities. Rumi captures its contradictions without resolving them, which is exactly right.
Book
The Discovery of India
Jawaharlal Nehru
Written in a British prison. A meditation on civilisation, culture, and what it means to belong somewhere ancient.
Book
Banker to the Poor
Muhammad Yunus
How a university professor started the phenomenon of microcredit and how a financial instrument helped in the emancipation of women.
Film
Children of Heaven
Majid Majidi, 1997
One of the greatest films ever made. Watch it. Then watch it again.
Film
Turtles Can Fly

About the beautiful and resilient Kurdish people who have endured immense suffering across a century. All the actors are refugee children.
Film
The Monk and the Gun

Film
Where Is My Friend's House?

One of the greatest storytellers who ever lived. This film contains more humanity per frame than most films manage in two hours.
Currently Reading

Housel's follow-up to The Psychology of Money. Where that book examined how we think about money, this one asks what never changes about human behaviour. The answer turns out to be more useful than most forecasts.
